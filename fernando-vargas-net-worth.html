Fernando Vargas | Career

Fernando made his professional debut on March 25, 1997. Vargas defeated Jorge Morales within only 56 seconds.

He easily defeated his next five opponents, fighting for only eight rounds in each of his first six fights.

When he earned his first world title, Fernando had won all 14 of his bouts by knockout.

In 1998, he won his first world title, the IBF light middleweight, by putting out Yori Boy Campas in seven rounds.

Vargas successfully defended his title against Howard Clarke, Raul Marquez, Winky Wright, and Ike Quartey throughout 1999.

In the year 2000, Vargas was defeated by Félix Trinidad for the title.

Fernando fought Sugar Shane Mosley twice in 2006. Vargas lost both of his battles to Mosley, and his last fight was in 2007 against Ricardo Mayorga.

He concluded his professional career with a 26-5 record.
