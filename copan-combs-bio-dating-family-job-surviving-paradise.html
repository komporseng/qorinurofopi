Copan Combs Job

Copan Combs is working as a senior admissions counselor at Oklahoma State University. He has been working in that role for 6 years.

He sometimes feels like a parent when he sees one of his students starting college. But, rather than 18 years, he only gets to know them for a little over a year. Senior admissions advisor Copan works for Oklahoma State University. He serves as many students’ and their families’ introduction to OSU.

Copan is constantly wearing America’s Brightest Orange, from his orange blazer all the way down to his orange shoes, whether he is visiting high schools in his region, which includes southwest Oklahoma and North Texas, or extending a warm welcome to prospective students during their visit to OSU. OSU has 25 admissions advisors who serve both the domestic and international markets.

Some have their headquarters there, such as Combs. There are counselors who reside in and establish ties with the cities where OSU enrolls a significant number of students, including the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, Tulsa, and Oklahoma City.

Copan is aware of the significance of his work. Additionally, he doesn’t only focus on recruiting; he also assists parents in understanding their possibilities for scholarships and financial aid and responds to any inquiries they may have about OSU or the college decision process.

“We have passion for a lot of things,” Copan said. “Obviously we’re passionate about OSU, but we’re proud to help out in general. There are so many things. If we don’t know an answer, we connect future students to someone on campus who does. For example, the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is confusing for a lot of people and we connect them with our Scholarships and Financial Aid Office team to help.”

He earned his degree in marketing and sports management, but after interning with OSU Athletics, he realized he wasn’t meant to be in the background. He wanted to be out meeting people and telling them about OSU.

The Oklahoma State University admissions counselor appeared on Season 14 of the Food Network cooking competition “Worst Cooks in America” which premiered in August 2018. A reality competition doesn’t seem like much of a leap, but a food program does, according to people who know him.
